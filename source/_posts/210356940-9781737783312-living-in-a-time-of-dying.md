          <p>
            Living in a Time of Dying is a spiritual manifesto for our
            times.&nbsp;It undertakes the sobering task of inviting us to feel
            the full weight of not only the inevitability of death, but the
            impending collapse of our human civilization, as evidenced through
            the social, psychospiritual, and ecological crises that we face in
            the 21st century on this planet Earth.&nbsp;Written for all those
            struggling with grief and fear about the future of our world, it
            offers a deeply poignant and compassionate vision for how to live in
            such times with integrity, courage, and grace: how to embrace the
            painful challenges of grief and rage without drowning in despair,
            how to hold hope alive without dissociating into fantasy, and how to
            root oneself in the nourishing and connecting power of love.
          </p>
          <p>&nbsp;</p>
          <p>
            The authors engage in a back-and-forth duologue of lyrical prose
            full of deep psychospiritual wisdom, incisive social commentary, and
            solemn ecological alarm.&nbsp;Informed by his 50 years of Taoist
            practice, Horden graciously invites the reader into a perception of
            self and world that is both immanently entwined within and
            transcendently outside of the attachments and identifications of
            this human world of space-time.&nbsp;Tauck penetratingly articulates
            the relationship between internal psychic strife and the social and
            historical traumas wrought by colonization and supremacisms in the
            external social world.&nbsp;Together they weave an intellectually
            cogent, psychospiritually profound, and emotionally tender vision
            for how to embrace this thing called living.
          </p>
