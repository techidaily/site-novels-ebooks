          <p>
            Serious students of Druidism and Paganism, as well as Celtic
            historians, are sure to include <i>Barddas</i> in their libraries.
            <i>Barddas</i> contains the only extant description of Bardo-Druidic
            Celtic philosophy. It is a metaphysical and spiritual description of
            beliefs handed down by word of mouth by Druidic initiates from
            before the Roman occupation of the British isles.
          </p>
          <p>
            Culled from 16th-century notes and compiled into book form,
            <i>Barddas</i> reveals a belief system with a wide range of
            influences, including Judeo-Christian and ancient Roman. Yet there
            are beliefs and views expressed within that appear to be unique to
            Celtic thought and oddly similar to Eastern traditions.
          </p>
          <p>
            On its publication in the 19th century, <i>Barddas</i> stirred
            controversy. Some critics claimed that it was completely made up or
            based on forgeries. Others defended it by pointing out similarities
            to other surviving Celtic documents with clear provenance.
          </p>
          <p>
            Photo-offset from the first printing, this edition of
            <i>Barddas</i> includes the original Welsh on verso pages with the
            English translation running on the recto pages.
          </p>
          <p>
            John Matthews, popular writer about all things Celtic, provides an
            introduction outlining the history and contemporary importance of
            <i>Barddas</i>.
          </p>
