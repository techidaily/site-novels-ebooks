          <b
            >Waking up or awakening means that the dream is over - the dream of
            unconsciousness we all spend most of our lives in - often a life of
            tensions, anguish, feeling unfulfilled and running after illusions
            which are just substitutes of what we are really looking for.</b
          ><br /><br />This little book is actually a big book, containing
          Osho's entire teaching from an early period of his public life and
          teachings. He takes us through three steps on the path to awakening,
          steps to help us to get out of our conditioning, our limitations and
          frustrations. "There are only three steps: freedom of consciousness,
          simplicity of mind, and emptiness of mind." For freedom of thought,
          for liberation of thought, and for the awakening of intelligence, the
          first thing, the first awareness that is needed is: “No thought is
          mine. No thought is mine.”<br /><br />
          While we read this book, Osho takes us through this experience of
          letting go of identification, allows us to get a glimpse what a life
          of awakening can be.
