          <b
            >A liberating exploration of the queer divine in deities and figures
            of myth and legend that opens pathways for LGBTQIA+ people to revere
            the divinity within and create an affirming spiritual practice.</b
          ><br /><br />A spiritual practice can be an intensely affirming thing,
          but for many LGBTQIA+ individuals, it is instead an alienating one.
          <i>Queer Devotion </i>seeks to open pathways for LBGTQIA+ people to
          connect with the divine by revering and celebrating the queer divinity
          in ourselves. By exploring the Queer Divine in existing gods,
          goddesses, and goddexes of mythology—Inanna, Hermes, Loki, Osiris, and
          more—we’ll resurrect the queer divine within ourselves through
          explorations and queered retellings of their stories. But we can also
          queer the idea of divinity <i>itself</i>, illuminating queer threads
          in Arthurian legend, European folklore, Victorian monsters, and
          witchcraft.<br /><br />Lastly, to build a queer devotional practice,
          Burgess offers exercises, reflection prompts, and ritual suggestions
          for you to build your own, personal practice of liberated,
          self-defined, and self-directed queer devotion.
