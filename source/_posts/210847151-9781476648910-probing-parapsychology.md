          <p>
            Parapsychology is a science made controversial by its subjects:
            extrasensory perception, psychokinesis (mind over matter) and
            disembodied minds, which imply life after death. Moreover, these
            parapsychological phenomena (called "psi") challenge physicalism,
            the philosophy that everything can be completely understood in terms
            of physics. This book is a snapshot of the parapsychological field,
            with essays written by authors of diverse academic backgrounds and
            experiences.
          </p>
          <p>
            Essays examine parapsychological phenomena from prehistory, through
            the founding of the science by intellectuals distressed by
            physicalism, to the postmodern present. It includes both
            experimental and theoretical evaluations of the phenomena.
            Parapsychology is a science which may overturn the philosophy which
            has dominated science since Newton and may inspire curious readers
            who are disheartened by the consequent denial of the spirit.
          </p>
