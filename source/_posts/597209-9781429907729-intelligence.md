              >In <i>Intelligence: The Creative Response to Now</i>, one of the
              twentieth century’s greatest spiritual teachers reveals a
              different approach to thinking about our brainpower. </b
            ><br /><br />In <i>Intelligence</i>, Osho challenges the common
            conception that the best way to promote intelligence is to train the
            intellect. Intellect is logical, he says; intelligence is
            paradoxical. Intellect takes things apart to see how they work;
            intelligence puts things together to see the functioning of the
            whole. Osho posits that when our education systems put too much
            emphasis on developing intellect, an imbalance is created and both
            the individual and the society suffer. It is only through
            intelligence that we can respond creatively to the challenges of a
            changing world.<br /><br />By exploring the distinction between
            intellect and intelligence, <i>Intelligence</i> encourages readers
            to be more aware of how they approach problems—logical, emotional,
            and practical—and how they solve them.<br /><br /><b
