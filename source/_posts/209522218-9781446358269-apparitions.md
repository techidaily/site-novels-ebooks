          unconscious part of the mind to bring to consciousness information
          that has been paranormally acquired—in crisis cases, for example. He

          the scenes in the unconscious part of the mind, and constructing the
          quasi-perceptual experience that eventually appears on the stage of
          consciousness, so that it embodies paranormal information in a
          symbolic way. Tyrrell first introduced the term out-of-body-experience
          in this book.<br />
