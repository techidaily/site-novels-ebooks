          <p>
            What makes us who we are? From a scientific viewpoint, any
            individual's existence is improbable at best. Consciousness as an
            actuality is inarguable; its nature, however, remains elusive. This
            work argues the view of self as a field of pure consciousness,
            debating the existence of a continuing self and drawing conclusions
            about this entity and its relation to the physical body and the
            physical world.
          </p>
          <p>
            Beginning with an exploration of the relationship between mind and
            matter, it discusses ostensible psi phenomena such as extra-sensory
            perception and psychokinesis and their implications for our
            understanding of the mind and the cosmos. Additional topics include
            the perennial mind-body problem; the role of consciousness in
            quantum mechanics (and conversely the role of quantum mechanics in
            the study of consciousness); the anthropic principle; and evidence
            for Intelligent Design. Quasi-religious questions such as the
            survival of consciousness after death are also addressed.
          </p>
