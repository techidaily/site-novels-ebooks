        <p>
          Author <b>S.E. Schlosser</b> has been telling stories since she was a

          full-length stories. A graduate of the Institute of Children's
          Literature and Rutgers University, she also created and maintains the
          website AmericanFolklore.net, where she shares a wealth of stories
          from all 50 states, some dating back to the origins of America. She
          lives outside New York City. <br /><br />Artist
          <b>Paul Hoffman </b>trained in painting and printmaking, with his
          first extensive illustration work on assignment in Egypt, drawing
          ancient wall reliefs for the University of Chicago. His work graces
          books of many genres - children's titles, textbooks, short story
          collections, natural history volumes, and numerous cookbooks. For
          <i>Spooky Massachusetts,</i> he employed a scratchboard technique and
          an active imagination. He lives in Greenfield, Massachusetts.
