          In mid argument with a loved one, as we stand unfairly accused, we
          would much rather be right than happy. As practising forgiveness
          raises our awareness of our attraction to and identification with the
          ego''s sneaky ways, our pain often increases. Some of these essays are
          reports from the eye of just that storm, accounts of my own fear,
          paralysis and mistaken belief in and attraction to a false, ego
          controlled self. But in each case, I got through by continuing to ask
          for help from the part of our mind that sees only our one true self
          and can never fail us. Today I am spending far less time in the eye of
          the storm and far more embracing the instant of release that reflects
          the real world available when we have forgiven all that would hurt us.
          You can, too.
