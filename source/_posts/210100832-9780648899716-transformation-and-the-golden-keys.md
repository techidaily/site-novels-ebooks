          <p>
            This autobiographical book draws on the wisdom and learnings of a
            lifetime of personal growth. The themes that run through the book
            are layers of personal stories drawn from a colorful family history,
            from many years as a veterinary surgeon, as facilitator in the
            adventure industry and as a trekking guide working with the Nepalese
            Gurkha soldiers. Beneath this are the psychological insights and
            wisdoms from an array of sources that relate to inner growth and
            psychology. The tone of the book is that of two friends on a trek
            sharing their personal stories, learnings, hopes and dreams. It is
            funny, sad, beautiful, brutal and honest. It goes on to discuss male
            and female initiation and the practices and methods of facilitating
            rites of passage programs and adventure work. Along the way, it
            discusses aspects of love, archetypal psychology, mental health,
            drug use, war, suicide, liminality, sacred processes and rituals,
            and most of all the miracle of life. It is a life-affirming book and
            by the end, the reader has a greater appreciation for the power of
            symbol and metaphor, for aspects of their inner world and their
            outer world, for the magic of cross-cultural interactions and
            ultimately the reader discovers a&nbsp;renewed love of self and of
            life. The book creates a paradigm shift within, a transformation,
            and the reader is drawn to consider an array of life skills and
            processes with which to negotiate life and the many adventures they
            will encounter on their life's journey
