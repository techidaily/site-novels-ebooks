          <b>A practical, hands-on guide to Mexican-American folk magic.</b
          ><br />
          &nbsp;<br /><i>American Brujeria</i>&nbsp;is about the fascinating
          blend of American and Mexican folk magic currently practiced by those
          living in the US but whose roots are steeped in Mexican
          culture.&nbsp;The author, who has named this tradition “American
          brujeria,” explores this magical system, while also&nbsp;offering
          practical advice on using it. American brujeria is a living, vital
          tradition that -- while it shares things in common with other folk
          magic traditions, such as American Conjure—also features its own
          unique traditions,&nbsp;as well as familiar ones,&nbsp;such as the
          veneration of saints, both canonized, such as Guadalupe, and folk
          saints like Santa Muerte.<br />
          &nbsp;<br /><i>American Brujeria</i>&nbsp;includes stories from Mexico
          (folk saints, the story of Guadalupe), the influence of Catholicism,
          the art of <i>limpias</i> (spiritual cleansings), spell casting, oil
          crafting, praying the rosary (in English and Spanish), making an altar
          to Guadalupe, using novena candle magic, crafting protective charms
          from saints’ medals, and more.<br />
