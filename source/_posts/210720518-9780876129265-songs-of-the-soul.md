          <p class="MsoNormal">
            Paramahansa Yogananda (1893–1952), author of the modern spiritual
            classic <i>Autobiography of a Yogi</i> and widely regarded as the
            father of Yoga in the West, contributed in far-reaching ways to a
            greater awareness and appreciation in the West of the spiritual
            wisdom of the East. Arriving in America from his native India in
            1920, he established Self-Realization Fellowship that same year, and
            devoted his life to helping people of all races and creeds to
            realize and express more fully in their lives the beauty, nobility
            and divinity of the human spirit.
          </p>
          <p class="MsoNormal">
            &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;
            Recognized from the beginning as a landmark work in its field,
            <i>Autobiography of a Yogi</i> has been in continuous print since
            its initial publication more than 75 years ago, and remains one of
            the most important, and most readable, works on Yoga and Eastern
            spiritual thought. Among Sri Yogananda’s other publications are
            acclaimed interpretations of the four Gospels, (<i
              >The Second Coming of Christ: The Resurrection of the Christ
              Within You</i
            >), the Bhagavad Gita (<i>God Talks With Arjuna</i>), and the
            Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam (<i
              >Wine of the Mystic: A Spiritual Interpretation</i
            >); a three-volume anthology series (<i
              >Collected Talks and Essays on Realizing God in Daily Life</i
            >); two collections of poetry and prayer; and numerous volumes of
            spiritual counsel.
          </p>
