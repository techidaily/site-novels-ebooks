          <b
            >A&nbsp;practical guide to the famed medieval book of
            pre-kabbalistic Jewish magic, freshly interpreted and revealed for
            the first time with instructions on how to use the spells.</b
          ><br /><br /><i>The Sword of Moses</i> is one of the earliest Jewish
          magic books, which describes a rite for adjuring angels to assist in
          controlling and wielding the "Sword of Moses" for magical purposes.
          The rite involves a short period of purification and then the adjuring
          of four sets of angels, each higher than the last. These angels in
          turn give the magician the power to control the Sword through a series
          of divine names that work as magical spells. The spells, 137 in all,
          have a wide variety of uses, including healing, harm, love, sex,
          exorcising demons, divination, and more.<br /><br />
          This work was first translated by Moses Gaster in 1896, but he removed
          many of the spells, making the text unusable for magic.
          <b><i>The Magic of the Sword of Moses</i></b> is the first book to
          show in detail, exactly how a magician can use the Sword—how to do the
          purification ritual, adjure the angels, and pronounce and use the
          divine names for each spell.
