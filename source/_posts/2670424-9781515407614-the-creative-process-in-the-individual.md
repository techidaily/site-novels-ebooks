          The Creative Process in the Individual scientifically explains "a
          sequence of creative action commencing with the formation of the globe
          and culminating in a vista of infinite possibilities attainable by
          every one who follows up the right line for their unfoldment." Troward
          asserts, "We each have a divine right of creation. Sharing in that
          divine power to create what is good will open up a wonderful vista of
          possibilities." Thomas Troward was an early New Thought writer who had
          an immense impact on those who would follow. Ernest Holmes, Frederick
          Bailes, Joseph Murphy, and Emmet Fox cited him as a major influence,
          and Genevieve Behrend was his student. It is impossible to
          overestimate his importance to the New Thought movement. His incisive
          fusion of Eastern and Western philosophy is unmatched.
