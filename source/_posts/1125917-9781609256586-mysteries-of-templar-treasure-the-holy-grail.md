          <p>
            Next to Rosslyn Chapel in Scotland, no other place on Earth holds as
            much esoteric symbolism as France's Rennes le Ch'teau. Its location
            and design are the subjects of countless rumors, myths, and legends.
            <i>Mysteries of Templar Treasure and the Holy Grail</i>, formerly
            published as <i>The Secrets of Rennes le Chateau</i>, delves into
            the reality behind the action and adventure of
            <i>The Da Vinci Code</i>. Rennes le Chateau has plenty of secrets:
            buried treasure, unsolved murders, supernatural powers, codes on
            parchments and tombstones, not to mention clues concealed in statues
            and paintings, enigmatic priests who controlled immense wealth, and
            secret societies that are still active today.
          </p>
          <p>
            The authors survey the arcane history and secrets of Rennes le
            Chateau, including its relationship to the Merovingian bloodline of
            Christ. The Chateau is a possible location of an immense treasure,
            such as a Templar, Cathar, or Priory of Sion hoard. The final
            resting place of a famous artifact like the Ark of the Covenant, the
            Spear of Longinus, the Emerald Tablets of Hermes Trismegistus- or
            even the Holy Grail.
          </p>
          <p>
            The authors also examine Rennes le Chateau's proximity to Cathar and
            Templar fortresses, its mystical layout, and its location on the
            same Paris meridian as so many other esoteric mysteries.
          </p>
          <p>
            Extensive appendices in the book offer possible solutions to secret
            cryptograms, point out odd connections and commonalities between
            Rennes le Chateau and J.R.R. Tolkien's <i>Lord of the Rings</i>, and
            suggest the possibility of fourth-dimension/tesseract implications.
          </p>
