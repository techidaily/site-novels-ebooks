          In 1945 the celebrated psi-researcher Harry Price published
          Poltergeist over England, popularizing the word poltergeist (German
          for "noisy ghost") and making famous the kind of physical haunting
          characterised by thrown objects, mysterious noises, and damage by fire
          or water. Now, for the first time, an astonishing array of historical
          Scottish poltergeist cases are gathered together, from the Middle Ages
          to the modern period, unearthing many episodes that have remained
          neglected for centuries. Some were no doubt hoaxes, but in others,
          multiple witnesses testified to disturbing events enacted over months.
          Whatever the true cause of the events, the historical evidence from
          Scotland suggests that poltergeist phenomena is undoubtedly real.
