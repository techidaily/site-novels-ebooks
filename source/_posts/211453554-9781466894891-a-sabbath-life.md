          <p>
            Poetic and provocative, a challenge to women to create more
            spiritually rich and balanced lives. A successful writer and a
            committed feminist, Kathleen Hirsch, at age forty, finds herself
            searching for something more. How, she asks, can women's lives be
            more spiritually alive and whole? Can we reclaim in our most
            productive years what we sacrificed to earlier ideas of success?
            What is the place of silence and creativity in our busy lives?<br /><br />Unable
            to trek to Tibet or retreat to a cabin in the woods, she enters a
            season of reflection in the midst of her everyday life. A career
            crisis, the sudden death of a brother, and the birth of her son, all
            in a year's time, deepen her probing. Hirsch examines the role of
            women's friendships and the definition of worthwhile work. Her inner
            pilgrimage gradually moves her to seek out a range of remarkable
            women who are consciously trying to live in balance. They lead her
            to bold conclusions that will inspire many women who are seeking
            realistic ways to live more multidimensional lives.Beautifully
            written, <i>A Sabbath Life</i> will serve as A Gift from the Sea for
            the twenty-first century.
