          Charles Ridley is known for having refined a version of biodynamic
          craniosacral work that is nonmedical and nonmechanical. In
          <i>Stillness</i>, he clarifies the three fundamental types of this
          work — biomechanical, functional, and biodynamic. He explains the
          requirements and pitfalls of each model, and how to discern the
          differences and similarities between them. He guides the practitioner
          experientially to explore what he is describing, and offers exercises
          drawn from his own practice to help therapists access directly the
          whole felt-body sense that connects each individual with the Breath of
          Life.
