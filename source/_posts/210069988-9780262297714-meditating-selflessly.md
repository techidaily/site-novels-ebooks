            >A guide to Zen meditative practice informed by the latest findings

          <p>
            This is not the usual kind of self-help book. Indeed, its major
            premise heeds a Zen master's advice to be <i>less</i> self-centered.
            Yes, it is "one more book of words about Zen," as the author
            concedes, yet this book explains meditative practices from the
            perspective of a "<i>neural</i> Zen." The latest findings in brain
            research inform its suggestions. In <i>Meditating Selflessly</i>,
            James Austin—Zen practitioner, neurologist, and author of three
            acclaimed books on Zen and neuroscience—guides readers toward that
            open awareness already awaiting them on the cushion and in the
            natural world.
          </p>
          <p>
            Austin offers concrete advice—often in a simplified
            question-and-answer format—about different ways to meditate. He
            clarifies both the concentrative and receptive styles of meditation.
            Drawing widely from the exciting new field of contemplative
            neuroscience, Austin helps resolve an ancient paradox: why both
            insight wisdom <i>and</i> selflessness arise simultaneously during
            enlightened states of consciousness.
          </p>
