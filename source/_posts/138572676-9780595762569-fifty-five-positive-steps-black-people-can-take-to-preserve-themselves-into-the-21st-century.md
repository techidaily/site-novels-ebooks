        <div accordion-content="" id="description">
          The American black is a unique individual, with a unique past and
          history that needs to be preserved.
          <p></p>
          <p>
            The American black is not a saint; neither is he a ghost. But he is
            flesh and blood, a human being that needs to be respected and
            treated fairly. But the treatment must first come through the manner
            in which the American black presents himself or herself to the
            world.
          </p>
          <p>
            <i
              >Fifty-Five Positive Steps Black People Can Take to Preserve
              Themselves into the 21st Century</i
            >
            provides psychological, physical and spiritual suggestions on how
            American blacks can empower themselves and therefore empower the
            race, through one another.
          </p>
          <p>
            Always with an eye on history and a hope for communal unity of some
            kind,
          </p>
          <p>Fifty-Five Steps</p>
          encourages American black people to do two things: to remember their
          past, where they come from, always; and to become well rounded people
          where they are now. And the two concepts are not mutually exclusive.
          Blacks can be all that they can be, love themselves and their history
          pridefully and still exist in the climate of modern America.
          <p></p>
          <p>
            Lastly, <i>Fifty-Five Steps</i> was written in reaction to a growing
            belief that "blackness" is dead or dying, that black Americans have
            been absorbed or assimilated. On the contrary,
            <i>Fifty-Five Steps</i> asks the black readers to love one another
            and to come together in a final stand for the integrity of essential
            blackness.
          </p>
