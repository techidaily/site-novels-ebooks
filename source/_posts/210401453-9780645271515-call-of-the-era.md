          <p>
            We each long to belong in our world. We experience belonging when we
            know who we are, why we are here, and how we are to live. Yet how
            can we experience belonging in a world which is characterized by the
            phrase, "all is not well?" Daily the news media portrays graphic
            images of circumstances which cause pain, fear, sorrow, and anger
            among people across the globe. Because all is not well, the Call of
            our era is toward social change. Significant social change requires
            a change in our identity. Why? Because our identity determines our
            purpose. Our purpose determines our values system. Our values system
            shapes the way we live within and act upon our world. Therefore, the
            deeper spiritual Call of the era is one of recasting our human way
            of being in the world. How can we generate such personal and
            collective inner change? In response, this little book of big ideas
            offers principles and practices for changing our personal and
            collective identity, with particular emphasis on the Western
            cultural identity.
