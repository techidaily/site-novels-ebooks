          Meditation can help children cope with the intensity of their feelings
          and the pressures in their lives—among family, with friends, at
          school. It gives even the youngest kids enhanced self-understanding,
          and is especially valuable for adolescents navigating the emotional
          peaks and valleys that emerge during that tricky period. That’s why
          parents, teachers, and anyone involved with young people will welcome
          this invaluable guide, which teaches them the techniques needed to
          work with children ages five to eighteen. The astonishing benefits
          range from relieving shyness, anxiety, and tension to reducing
          hyperactivity, aggression, and impatience. Meditation has also proved
          useful in treating asthma, insomnia, and depression, in improving
          concentration and creativity, and in establishing balance throughout a
          child’s life.
