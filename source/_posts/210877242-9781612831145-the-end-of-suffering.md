          <b
            >This spiritual inquiry into the nature of truth draws on Buddhism
            and quantum physics to liberate us from limited understandings of
            ourselves and others.</b
          ><br /><br />The hopeful teaching of this book is that while everybody
          suffers, most of this suffering is unnecessaryâ€”it can be overcome. The
          belief that things must be either true or untrue leads us to think in
          terms of polarities: good or evil, right or wrong. This friend-or-foe
          approach may seem to make life easier, but in
          <i>The End of Suffering</i>, Russell Targ and J. J. Hurtak assert that
          this worldview only increases our experience of suffering.<br /><br />In
          an effort to overcome the polarity of opposites and the accompanying
          suffering, Targ and Hurtak combine the wisdom of the East with the
          findings of quantum physics, uncovering a middle ground that shows
          opposing sides are really the same.<br /><br />Buddha taught us to
          live a helpful and compassionate life and to surrender our ego to the
          peace of spaciousness. The middle path of Buddhism also shows that
          things may be neither true nor not true, or both true and untrue.
          <i>The End of Suffering</i> puts the perceived opposites of Buddhism
          and physics together, showing step-by-step how we can learn to
          surrender the story of who we think we are and experience an end to
          our suffering.
