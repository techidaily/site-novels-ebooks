          Dr Colm O'Connor, a clinical psychologist and couples therapist, has
          for over twenty years explored the emotional lives of couples and
          researched hundreds of cases of couples in distress.Looking beneath
          the surface of everyday complaints, The Courage to Love reveals those
          insights and shows how we seek solutions to life's most essential
          questions in close relationships. It answers common questions that we
          often ask such as: 'Why do we constantly argue about trivial things?'
          'Why do we have to win an argument at all?' 'Why is it that we often
          forget what it is we end up fighting about?' 'How is it that love can
          deteriorate into abuse?'In answering these kinds of questions, Dr
          O'Connor shows what is ultimately at stake for people in winning an
          argument, starting a fight, proving a point, triumphing in divorce, or
          abusing a lover, and presents a range of solutions that are not about
          how to control relationships but how to inhabit them.Painting a
          dramatic portrait of love as a heroic response to human vulnerability,
          The Courage to Love shines new light on how relationship breakdown
          happens and provides a guide for getting back on track.
