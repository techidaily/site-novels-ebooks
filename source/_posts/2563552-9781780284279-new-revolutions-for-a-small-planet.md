          According to sociologist Kingsley L. Dennis, our current economic woes
          are part of a larger shift in the way our global systems function. In
          his recent book, New Revolutions for a Small Planet (Watkins, November
          2012), Dennis provides a strong dose of hope for young adults
          everywhere—especially those hit hardest by the current economic
          crisis. According to Dennis, we are witnessing the last throes of the
          industrial revolution model and mindset. "The signs are all around
          us," he says, pointing to the rise of decentralized communications,
          the growth of NGOs and volunteerism, and rising social unrest against
          dysfunctional political systems. "These are all harbingers of the next
          phase of our socio-cultural evolution." New Revolutions looks at what
          young people can expect in the years ahead: The birth of the ‘empathic
          mind,’ which is heralded by greater global interconnections, networked
          communication, and a participatory culture; A burgeoning sense of a
          dynamic, enchanting cosmos; and the increased urge to find personal
          meaning and fulfillment in life; Greater awareness of how new
          innovations will lead to creative new ways of thinking that will solve
          the world’s current problems; New systems and models that will emerge
          to eventually make the old models obsolete. In his conclusion, Dennis
          writes that "Revolutions in communications and energy technology go
          together with our shifting forms of consciousness," and that "life
          upon this planet is about to undergo a period of transition that will
          test our capacity to re-adapt and re-emerge on an unprecedented
          scale."
