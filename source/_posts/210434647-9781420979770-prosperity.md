          First published in 1936, “Prosperity: Spiritual Secrets to an Abundant
          Life”, by the celebrated New Thought mystic and author Charles
          Fillmore, is the pioneering self-help guide that guides the reader in
          a transformation from rags to riches. Born in Minnesota in 1854,
          Fillmore received little formal education, but was a voracious reader
          and studied classic literature, Eastern religions, metaphysics, and
          works on spiritualism and the Bible. In 1886, Fillmore and his wife
          and children moved to Kansas City, Missouri and began attending New
          Thought classes and soon began publishing a magazine called “Modern
          Thought”, which contained the ideas of the New Thought movement on
          spirituality, prayer, and mysticism. They went on to found the Unity
          Church and Fillmore published many books on his belief in metaphysics
          and the power of prayer and positive thinking. In “Prosperity”,
          Fillmore sets out twelve lessons to help the reader find financial
          wealth and abundance, along with a personal sense of purpose and
          strength. Fillmore was an early proponent of the idea that our state
          of mind affects our physical reality and his powerful ideas on the
          importance of positive thinking and emotions have inspired and helped
          countless readers.
