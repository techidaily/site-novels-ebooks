        <div accordion-content="" id="description">
          Many people believe that they have experienced paranormal phenomena
          and others claim to possess psychic abilities. For the past hundred
          years or so, researchers have undertaken systematic and scientific
          work into these alleged experiences and abilities. This collection of
          articles provides readers with a general sense of the methods used in
          this research, the findings that have been obtained and the
          controversies generated by this work. They cover a wide range of
          issues, including the psychology of paranormal belief, investigations
          into ghosts and hauntings, laboratory research into extra-sensory
          perception and psychokinesis, and controlled tests of psychics and
          mediums. An introductory essay sets each of the selected papers in
          context and provides additional references for those wishing to delve
          deeper into the issues surrounding each of the areas covered.
