          <p>
            Contemporary parapsychology tends to be preoccupied with ESP
            (telepathy, clairvoyance, precognition) and psychokinesis. In
            contrast, this cutting-edge anthology assembles an international
            team of experts from the fields of psychology, parapsychology,
            philosophy, anthropology and neuroscience to examine critically what
            is referred to as the <i>survival hypothesis</i>: the tentative
            statement or prediction that some aspect of our personhood (e.g.,
            consciousness) persists subsequent to the death of the physical
            body.
          </p>
          <p>
            The appraisal of the survival hypothesis will be restricted to the
            phenomenon of mediumship; that is, humans who ostensibly communicate
            with the deceased. The book has been divided into four main
            sections: Explanation and Belief; Culture, Psychopathology and
            Psychotherapy; Empirical Approaches; The Present and Future. The
            issue of postmortem survival is supremely relevant to us all because
            the human encounter with death is, of course, a certainty.
          </p>
