          Whilst the concept of karma is derived from ancient Eastern religious
          tradition, the word is very much part of contemporary vocabulary. We
          speak of karma in the sense of individuals getting 'what they deserve'
          – usually in a negative sense. But is karma a retributive law or is it
          based on healing and love?In his latest thought-provoking work, Dr Bob
          Woodward investigates such issues in conversation with his spirit
          guides. Beginning with the most basic question, 'What is karma?', they
          discuss how this law of destiny relates to illness, human freedom,
          relationships and individual development – but also to major crises on
          the world stage, such as the coronavirus pandemic, wars and conflict,
          global warming and climate change. They also examine how such key
          matters might relate to 'world karma' rather than personal
          destiny.Bob's spiritual guides speak of the law of karma as being,
          '…filled with compassion. It is not a law which makes people or beings
          subservient to some almighty and dictatorial power. It is a law which
          is generated by love itself, and is there to bring order, recompense
          and compassion to all beings and their mutual relationships.' Karma in
          Human Life offers answers to many puzzles and enigmas relating to
          modern-day life on earth.
