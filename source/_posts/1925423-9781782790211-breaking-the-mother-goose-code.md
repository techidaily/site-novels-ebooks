          Who was Mother Goose? Where did she come from, and when? Although
          she’s one of the most beloved characters in Western literature, Mother
          Goose’s origins have seemed lost in the mists of time. Several have
          tried to pin her down, claiming she was the mother of Charlemagne, the
          wife of Clovis (King of the Franks), the Queen of Sheba, or even
          Elizabeth Goose of Boston, Massachusetts. Others think she’s related
          to mysterious goose-footed statues in old French churches called
          “Queen Pedauque.” This book delves deeply into the surviving evidence
          for Mother Goose’s origins – from her nursery rhymes and fairy tales
          as well as from relevant historical, mythological, and anthropological
          data. Until now, no one has ever confidently identified this
          intriguing yet elusive literary figure. So who was the real Mother
          Goose? The answer might surprise you.
