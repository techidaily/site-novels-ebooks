          <p>
            Practicing Western astrologer Agneta Borstein uses an East-West
            astrological approach to the lunar nodes, their role in synastry,

            addition, her familiarity with the Vedic system and its mythology
            brings a unique perspective to her interpretation of the nodes.
          </p>
          <p>
            This is a revised and expanded edition of
            <i>The Moon's Nodes. A Churning Process of the Soul</i> first
            published in 2004 expands on the nodes in synastry and their roles
            in our relationships. It presents the Hindu myths related to the
            nodes with an emphasis on the goddesses as consorts to the male
            deities and how their stories connect to the Lunar nodes.
          </p>
          <p>
            Borstein presents the fundamental facts about the Moon's Nodes and
            includes a discussion of the True and Mean Node and information on
            the nodal cycle in transit, natal signs and houses of the nodes and
            their meaning, especially in how to move from the wounded soul,
            represented by the South Node, to higher consciousness, represented
            by the North Node.
          </p>
          <p>
            Includes a fascinating synastry study of Angelina, our modern day
            goddess, and her consort Brad Pitt with an interpretation of the
            ties that knot them together.
