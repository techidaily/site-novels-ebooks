              >A modern exploration of ancient wisdom relating to psychoactive

            >
          </p>
          <p>
            The ancient ritual drink used in religious ceremonies and known as
            soma in India and as haoma in the Zoroastrian tradition is praised
            in the highest terms - as a kind of deity - in both Zoroastrian and
            Vedic texts, which date from around 1,700 - 1,500 BCE.
          </p>
          <p>
            It is said to provide health, power, wisdom and even immortality.
          </p>
          <p>
            Many theories have been published about the possible botanical
            identity of this 'nectar of immortality', a plant which appears to
            have psychedelic/entheogenic properties.
          </p>
          <p>
            Matthew Clark spent several years researching and travelling widely
            in his quest of soma and in his fascinating, original and highly
            readable book, Clark reviews scholarly research, explores mythology
            and ritual and shares his extensive knowledge of psychoactive plants
            and fungi.
          </p>
          <p>
            The author suggests that the visionary soma drink was based on
            analogues of ayahuasca, using a variety of plants, some of which can
            now be identified.
          </p>
