          <p>
            Dream books that guide readers to work with their dreams invariably
            ask them to write their dreams down, or perhaps record them.&nbsp;<i
              >The Art of Dreaming</i
            >&nbsp;stands apart from all other dream books in that it invites
            readers to work with their dreams in whatever medium is most natural
            and beneficial to them. For some, that might in fact be writing or
            talking, but for others it might be drawing or painting or working
            in clay or dancing or dramatizing or recreating movement or
            maskmaking or working in multimedia or creating poetry. This book is
            a beautiful integration of dreaming and creativity, one that takes
            readers to a place where they can work with both the essential and
            deep messages from their dreams.
          </p>
          <p>
            The book makes use of illustrative icons to clearly indicate to
            readers the art medium for each activity, enabling them to go
            directly to the type of activity that most appeals to them.
          </p>

            therapist, details a multitude of ways to decipher your
            dreams…describes visual, vocal, and theatrical methods for cracking
