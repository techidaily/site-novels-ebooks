            <strong
              >Old Crow is a post-WWII story told through the eyes of a tender
              five year old boy.</strong
            >
            In between days filled with coon hunting with the hounds, swimming
            in the river, and egging the family hen house with his older brother
            were dark, terrifying nights. Arthur's father returned home from
            war, but he relived his anguish daily in his mind. Before PTSD was
            even recognized as such, Arthur's father self-medicated his
            undiagnosed terror with alcohol, then unleashed his own brand of
            violence and pain onto his wife and children. As such, the two
            dirty-faced, inseparable brothers found reprieve and temporary days
            of innocence at their grandparents' Oklahoma farm, The Duffy Place,
            where boys could be boys. The brothers' resilience combined with the
            bravery and love of their mother, who mitigated the daily violence
            with self-sacrifice, is nothing short of inspirational.
