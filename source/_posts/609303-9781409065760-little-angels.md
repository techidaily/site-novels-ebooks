          The real-life stories of the novice monks in
          <i>Little Angels</i> reflect the lives of many youths in rural
          Thailand who are trapped in the vicious cycle of poverty, broken
          homes, illiteracy and drug abuse. When all else fails, Buddhism
          becomes their last resort: providing them with physical shelter and
          spiritual refuge. It heals their childhood traumas and gives them a
          moral framework for living and a better outlook on life. Each
          individual story, heartrending as it may be, subtly shows what Phra
          Peter sees and hopes to show to others: the 'human face' of Thai
          Buddhism.
