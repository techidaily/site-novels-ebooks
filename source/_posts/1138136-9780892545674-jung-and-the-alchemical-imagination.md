        <div accordion-content="" id="description">
          <i>Jung and the Alchemical Imagination</i> illustrates the spiritual
          nature of Jungian psychology and the debt it owes to the tradition of
          esoteric religion. Unlike other books on Jung and alchemy which
          contain a psychological interpretation of alchemical material, this
          work uses alchemy to understand the three cornerstones of Jungian
          spirituality--the self, the transcendent function, and active
          imagination. <br /><br />Through the interpretation of alchemical
          imagery, Raff explains the nature of these three concepts and
          illustrates how together they form a new model of contemporary Western
          spirituality. This book is also unique in selecting alchemical texts
          for analysis that are relatively unknown and which, for the most part,
          have never been interpreted. In addition, he presents two new
          concepts--the ally and the psychoid realm. Through the addition of
          these ideas, and the new understanding that they offer, it is possible
          to apply alchemical imagery to transpsychic experience/ that is, to a
          world of spirits which may not be reduced to psychological concepts.
          By including this realm in the study of alchemy and Jungian thought,
          it is possible to gain insights into the nature of visionary and
          ecstatic experiences that form part of the path of individuation--the
          road to completion.
