          <p>
            This is an uncompromising and provocative book about non-duality.
          </p>
          <p>
            In liberation it is seen that all phenomena simply arise in
            awareness with no person mediating them. Purpose, religion and paths
            of spiritual development all lose their meaning when it is seen that
            there is no one who exercises choice.
          </p>
          <p>
            “The most common misconception about liberation is that it is
            something an individual can gain. But liberation is a loss—the loss
            of the sense that there ever was a separate individual who could
            choose to do something to bring about liberation. When it is seen
            that there is no separation, the sense of vulnerability and fear
            that attaches to the individual falls away ... then life is simply
            lived and relaxation takes place. There is a sense of ease with
            whatever is the case and an end to grasping for what might be."<br />
            —From <i>I Hope You Die Soon</i>
          </p>
