          To the casual observer, they're just a pack of playing cards. But to

          comprehend their esoteric truths.<br />

          <i>The Tarot of the Bohemians</i>&nbsp;provides a theological element
          to the work begun by Antoine Court de Gébelin, Etteila, and Éliphas
          Lévi, and advanced by A. E. Waite, the distinguished scholar and
          designer of the most widely known deck. Geared toward initiates, its
          topics include an introduction to the study of the Tarot as well as
          their&nbsp;symbolism and applications. This revised edition includes a
          Preface by Waite&nbsp;in addition to illustrative plates, charts, and
          tables.
