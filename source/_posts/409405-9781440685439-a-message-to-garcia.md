          ><br /><br />
          <i>A Message to Garcia</i> touched so profound a chord in readers
          that, after its publication in 1899, it became one of the most widely
          read works in human history. <i>A Message to Garcia</i> tells the
          story of an American soldier charged with delivering a critical
          message to a leader of Cuban rebel forces during the Spanish American
          War. In Hubbard’s account, he delivered the urgent missive with no
          questions asked, no complaining, and no hedging. The enduring and
          almost unbelievably simple message of the essay is this: When asked to
          perform a task, don’t ask <i>How.</i>..? or <i>Why.</i>..? or
          <i
            >Wouldn’t it be better if,/I . . . ? Just do it—and you will become
            more valued and respected than you ever imagined possible.<br /><br
