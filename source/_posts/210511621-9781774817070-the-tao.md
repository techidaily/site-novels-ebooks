            The Tao that can be trodden is not the enduring and unchanging Tao.

            (Conceived of as) having no name, it is the Originator of heaven and
            earth; (conceived of as) having a name, it is the Mother of all

          <p>Always without desire we must be found,</p>
          <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;If its deep mystery we would sound;</p>
          <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;But if desire always within us be,</p>
          <p>&nbsp;&nbsp;Its outer fringe is all that we shall see.</p>
          <p><br /></p>
          <p>
            Under these two aspects, it is really the same; but as development
            takes place, it receives the different names. Together we call them
            the Mystery. Where the Mystery is the deepest is the gate of all
            that is subtle and wonderful.
          </p>
          <p><br /></p>
          <p>
            All in the world know the beauty of the beautiful, and in doing this
            they have (the idea of) what ugliness is; they all know the skill of
            the skilful, and in doing this they have (the idea of) what the want
            of skill is.
