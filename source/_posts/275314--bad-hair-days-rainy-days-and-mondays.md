          <p>
            This book offers a month’s worth of wisdom, advice, and
            encouragement for women in the form of 31 short daily meditational
            readings, written by award-winning teacher and author Cynthia Bond
            Hopson. The life lessons include:<br />· Nothing is worth more than
            this day<br />· Practice saying no<br />· Do not give in to
            flattery<br />· Take care of yourself – Pain is information<br />·
            Listen to the children<br />· There is a lesson in every failure<br />·
            Remember, age ain’t nothing but a number<br />· Three giggles a day
            will keep the doctor away
          </p>
          <p>
            The book begins with a brief, inspirational introduction giving
            suggestions for how to use the book. The 31 readings consist of a
            quotation, a Scripture reading, the meditation itself, and a closing
            prayer. The author suggests including a space after each lesson for
            the reader to add her own reflections and prayer, but I don’t
            believe this is necessary.
          </p>
          <p>Hopson’s style is conversational, humorous, and appealing.</p>
          <p>
            Dr. Cynthia Bond Hopson is a freelance writer and associate
            professor of journalism at the University of Memphis. She has
            written thought-provoking and inspirational columns, feature
            articles, and speeches. Her first book, Wiggle Tales, was a
            self-published collection of her columns from the Brownsville
            States-Graphic. She has been nominated for teaching excellence and
            has twice been named to the Who’s Who Among American Teachers. She
            founded Touched by Grace Professional Communications Ministry in
            2002 as an outlet for her consulting, public speaking,
            speechwriting, and vocal talents.
          </p>
          <p>
            The author is African American, and this proposal has been reviewed
            by and received endorsement from the African American Initiative
            Group.
