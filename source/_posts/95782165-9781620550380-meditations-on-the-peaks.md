        <div accordion-content="" id="description">
          Evola articulates the close relationship between the physical rigors
          of mountain climbing and the ascent of the initiate toward
          self-transcendence. <br />Julius Evola, a leading exponent of esoteric
          thought, was also an ardent mountain climber who personally scaled the
          peaks of the Tyrols, Alps, and Dolomites. For Evola the physical
          conquest of a mountain, with all the courage, self-transcendence and
          mental lucidity that it entails, becomes an inseparable and
          complementary part of spiritual awakening. It is no coincidence that
          many ancient cultures chose mountains as the abodes of their gods and
          considered the rigorous ascent of peaks as the task of heroes and
          initiates. In modern times, which tend to suffocate the heroic with
          naked self interest, the mountain still forms part of the profound
          dimension of spirit where the soul finds within itself more than what
          it thought itself to be. In <i>Meditations on the Peaks</i>, Evola
          combines recollections of his own experiences with reflections on
          other inspirational men and women who shared his view of the
          transcendent greatness of mountains.
