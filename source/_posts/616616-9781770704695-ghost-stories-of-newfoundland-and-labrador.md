          <p>
            Newfoundland and Labrador has tales of the supernatural that date
            back centuries, to a time before Canada even existed as a nation.
            Here the ghosts not only lurk in old houses and forlorn cemeteries,
            they come up out of the sea to walk the decks of ships before the
            eyes of terrified crewmen. They lament out on the ice where
            seventy-seven men perished in the Newfoundland Sealing Disaster of
            1914. And in St. Johns the courthouse is said to be haunted by the
            ghost of Catherine Snow, who was hanged in 1834 for the murder of
            her husband.
          </p>
