          <b
            >Celebrated for her indelible, Oscar-caliber performances in some of
            the most memorable films of the 1980s and 1990s, Debra Winger, in
            <i>Undiscovered</i>, her first book, demonstrates that her creative
            range extends from screen to page. Here is an intimate glimpse of an
            artist marvelously wide-ranging in her gifts. </b
          ><br /><br />In fact, as this beguiling book reveals, Winger is that
          rare star who dared to resist the all-consuming industry that is
          Hollywood becoming her entire reason for being. "I love the work," she
          states, "and don't much care for the business." Yet she cares deeply
          for the people who have inspired her. We meet them (most famously,
          James Bridges, Bernardo Bertolucci; most dearly, her mother, husband,
          and sons) here, as Winger passionately makes her case for forging a
          life beyond acting -- and shows how she has done just that. <br />
          <br />Winger's screen performances have long been celebrated for their
          breathtaking emotional range, a quality that shines through in these
          pages. "When I was little," she writes, "someone told me that when you
          age, you turn into the person you were all your life." In this
          intriguing mix of reminiscence, poetry, storytelling, and insightful
          observation, a portrait of a life well-lived is strikingly rendered.
