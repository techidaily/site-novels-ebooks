            ><i>Mexican Magic</i>&nbsp;shares spells and recipes deeply rooted

          ><br /><br />
          “Some are born with a star, while others are born starry.”<br />
          &nbsp;<br />
          This <i>dicho </i>(saying) refers to the Mexican belief that good luck
          is a matter of fate—something you are born with or not. Mexicans
          traditionally attribute their good or bad luck to a greater force, to
          God’s will, even to the stars in the sky. Being born with a star is a
          blessing.<br />
          &nbsp;<br />
          While some gain their luck through fate, Laura Davila believes even
          more in faith, virtue, and purpose. Some people are born with a
          natural gift for magic, but many others are compelled toward magic by
          life experience. The best <i>brujos </i>and magical people are not
          those who necessarily started off in perfect circumstances but those
          who looked at magic as a skill to be mastered.<br />
          &nbsp;<br /><i>Mexican Magic</i> offers an overview of magic and
          spells from across Mexico for daily use. Although the book’s spells
          may be practiced by anyone, they are deeply rooted in Mexican folk
          beliefs and magic. Featuring magical recipes, spells, tips, and advice
          for a wide variety of intentions, including love, lust, sex, good
          luck, money, protection, commerce, gambling, justice, pregnancy,
          travel, education, and more, <i>Mexican Magi</i>c also offers
          direction on how to be a magical person and live a magical life.
