          This groundbreaking work examines the role of women in the Western
          healing traditions. Drawing on the disciplines of history,
          anthropology, botany, archaeology, and the behavioral sciences, Jeanne
          Achterberg discusses the ancient cultures in which women worked as
          independent and honored healers; the persecution of women healers in
          the witch hunts of the Middle Ages; the development of midwifery and
          nursing as women's professions in the nineteenth century; and the
          current role of women and the state of the healing arts, as a time of
          crisis in the health-care professions coincides with the reemergence
          of feminine values.
