        <div accordion-content="" id="description">
          A study of the new scientific understanding of consciousness and the
          mind as a fifth dimension of reality <br /><br />• Introduces the
          existence of a fifth dimension--one of mind--an inner- or hyperspace
          where time is transcended <br /><br />• Shows how the barrier of the
          speed of light is actually a gateway demarking the fifth dimension
          <br /><br />Since the introduction of Descartes’ dualism in the
          seventeenth century, the mind and the physical world have been viewed
          as disconnected entities. Yet qualities of mind such as awareness,
          purposeful action, organization, design, and even decision-making are
          present within the structure of matter and within the dimensions of
          space and time.<br /><br />
          The space-time continuum of scientists generally ignores the realm of
          the mind, though phenomena such as imaginary numbers, used by Einstein
          to combine space with time, are concepts that only exist in the mind.
          Marc Seifer contends that the inadequacy of four-dimensional models to
          account for our experience of mental phenomena points to the
          consciousness of the mind as a higher organizing principle, a fifth
          dimension where thoughts are as real and quantifiable as our familiar
          physical world. He shows that because thought enables us to move
          backward and forward through time--reflecting on the past and making
          plans for the future--this fifth dimension of mind breaks the laws of
          relativity, thereby transcending the speed of light. His extensive
          study of this fifth dimension ranges from relativity and ether theory
          to precognition, telepathy, and synchronicity, all from the
          perspective of the conscious universe.
