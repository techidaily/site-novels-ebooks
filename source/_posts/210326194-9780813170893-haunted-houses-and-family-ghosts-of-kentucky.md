          mansions, log cabins, bathrooms, furniture, hotels, and distilleries,
          as well as reports of eerie visitations from passed-on grandmothers,
          husbands, daughters, uncles, cousins, babies, slaves, Civil War
          soldiers, dogs, sheep, and even wildcats.<br /><br />Almost every
          county in Kentucky is represented. Though the book emphasizes the
          stories themselves, Montell offers an introduction discussing how
          local history, and local character, are communicated across the
          generations in these colorful stories.
