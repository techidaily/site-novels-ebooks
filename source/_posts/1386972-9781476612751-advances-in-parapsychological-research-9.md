        <p>
          <b>Stanley Krippner</b>, Ph.D., former director of the Maimonides

          England, Armidale, NSW, Australia. His primary research interests are
          in the area of altered states of consciousness, mainly focused on
          shamanism, the conceptual problem of consciousness, and mediumship.
          <b></b><b>Julie Beischel</b> is co-founder and director of research at
          the Windbridge Institute for Applied Research in Human Potential in
          Tucson, Arizona. <b></b>Consulting and clinical psychologist
          <b>Harris L. Friedman</b>, Ph.D. is an associated distinguished
          consulting professor at the California Institute of Integral Studies,
          an associated professor at the Salomons Institute for Applied
          Psychology at Canterbury Christ Church University (UK), and a visiting
          scholar at Harvard University. He retired as a research professor of
          psychology at the University of Florida. <b></b
          ><b>Cheryl L. Fracasso</b>, Ph.D., LMHC is a licensed mental health
          counselor, certified clinical trauma intervention specialist, and
          owner of ShangriLa Wellness Center, a holistic healthcare center. She
          served on the faculty at the University of Phoenix for over 9 years
          teaching undergraduate psychology classes.
